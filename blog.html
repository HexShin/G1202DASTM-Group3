Destination
Historical landmark| Daraga, Albay Cagsawa Ruins

The church was originally built in the town of Cagsawa in 1587 but was burned down and destroyed by Dutch pirates in 1636. It was rebuilt in 1724 by Fr. Francisco Blanco, but was destroyed again, along with the town of Cagsawa, on February 1, 1814, during the eruption of the Mayon Volcano.

https://albay.gov.ph/?page_id=6493

Destination
Park| Legazpi City, Albay Ligñon Hill

Ligñon Hill is a 156-meter hill that offers panoramic views of Legazpi City, the Albay Gulf, and the Mayon Volcano. It is a popular tourist destination and is known for its stunning views, especially of the Mayon Volcano. The hill is also home to a lighthouse, a park, and a restaurant.

Destination
Church| Daraga, Albay Daraga Church

Our Lady of the Gate Parish Church, is a captivating blend of Baroque and Oriental architecture. The centuries-old church boasts intricate carvings and a serene interior with religious artifacts. Beyond its historical significance, the church offers a breathtaking view of the majestic Mayon Volcano and the surrounding landscapes, making it a must-visit destination that seamlessly combines spiritual and visual appeal.

https://www.flickr.com/photos/ronlo_images/14395585862

Destination
Lake| Camalig, Albay Sumlang Lake

Whether you seek serenity, adventure, or a glimpse into local life, Sumlang Lake awaits with open arms. Its beauty whispers in the rustle of leaves, its spirit dances in the ripples of the water, and its charm lingers long after your visit, a reflection of Mayon's majesty etched in your heart.

Park| Bacacay, Albay Cagraray Eco Park Bacacay Albay

The park offers well-maintained trails, scenic viewpoints of the Pacific Ocean, and exciting adventures like zip-lining and canopy walks. Food
Pilinuts

Albay is known for its homegrown premium pilinuts. The usual favorites are crispy candies pilinuts with honey, roasted and salted pili, mazapan, butternuts, caramelitos, pastillas and sesame seed coated pilinuts.

Bicol Express

Food
Pinangat

Pinangat is a version of laing, a famous native chili-hot dish of a little town of Camalig,Albay which is a blend of ground pork, coconut milk, diced shrimps, and chili wrapped in gabi leaves. This dish is well-known among Bicolanos because of its distinctive taste that a Bicolano could perfect.

https://albay.gov.ph/?page_id=6500

Food
Ligao’s Rice Puto

Made of ground rice with coconut candy as a filling, Ligao’s Rice Puto is the most popular rice delicacy of its kind in Albay. Wrapped in a banana leaf, both children and adults can enjoy this sweet specialty of Ligao City.

Event
Albay, Philippines Magayon Festival

Held every month of May, Magayon Festival is derived from the popular legend of Mayon Volcano to honor the province's bounty and beauty. One of the highlights of this event is the stage reenactment of the tragic love story of Daragang Magayon and Panganoron whose fatal deaths gave birth to the volcano.

https://albay.gov.ph/?page_id=18018

Event
Legazpi City, Albay Ibalong Festival

A week-long festivity recounting the brave exploits of the heroes of Ibalong or prehistoric Bicol. The festival is highlighted by a street parade recreating the classic battles between heroes and beasts of Ibalong.

https://www.festivalscape.com/philippines/albay/ibalong-festival/

Event
Tabaco City, Albay Tabak Festival

A celebration showcasing the artistry of Tabaquenos in producing their very own cutleries with hard labor. It came from the word “tabak ko! tabak ko!” which means “ my sword, my sword!”. Tabaco City is known for having the “blacksmiths” in Barangay Cobo.

https://albay.gov.ph/?festivals_of_albay=tabak-festival-tabaco-albay

Albay is a beautiful province in the Bicol Region, known to the world as the home of Mt. Mayon, the volcano with a perfect shape of a cone. It is a relatively unspoiled region in south Luzon, with many natural wonders including beaches, springs, caves and waterfalls. Albay offers almost endless discoveries, especially for the nature-loving traveler.
